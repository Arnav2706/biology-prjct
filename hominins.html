Rise of Hominins

The hominins are a group of species that include modern humans, our direct ancestors, and other extinct relatives. This lineage began around 7 million years ago with species that showed early signs of bipedalism, allowing them to walk upright on two legs.

Evolutionary Milestones

Hominins like Australopithecus and Paranthropus adapted to diverse environments in Africa. These early human ancestors developed tools, social structures, and communication methods that set the stage for the evolution of the genus Homo.
