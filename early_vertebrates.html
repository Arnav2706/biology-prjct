Evolution of Early Vertebrates

About 500 million years ago, the first vertebrates appeared. These early fish-like creatures developed a backbone, providing structural support and protection for their nervous system. The evolution of vertebrates allowed for greater mobility and adaptability in different environments.

Transition to Land

The move from water to land was a critical milestone in evolution. The development of limbs and lungs allowed some vertebrates to explore terrestrial environments, leading to the rise of amphibians, reptiles, and eventually mammals.

Emergence of Hominin Ancestors

The lineage leading to humans diverged from other primates around 7 million years ago. Early hominins, like Sahelanthropus, displayed bipedalism, marking the beginning of human evolution. These ancestors laid the groundwork for the development of the genus Homo.
